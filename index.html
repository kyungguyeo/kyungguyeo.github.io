HealthGrader

Gaining insights into public health from Twitter based on Social Determinants of Health

HealthGrader uses Twitter data to help public health officials understand how social determinants of health are linked with a range of chronic diseases.

What are Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)?

"The conditions in the environment in which people are born, live, learn, work, play, worship, and age that affect a wide range of health, functioning, and quality-of-life outcomes and risks. -HealthyActions"


Motivation

SDOH drive health 4X as much as clinical care

Research

Commercial and government health insurers have an interest in managing SDOH.


Issue

Tracking SDOH is difficult and relies on voluntary surveys conducted in clinics or households.

Our workflow of getting insights from Twitter activities

Our Solution

HealthGrader supplements surveys with Twitter users who self-report SDOH.

With the profile data of these high and low risk users, we train deep learning models to predict SDOH risk scores.

In this tool, we demonstrate how SDOH risk scores vary between groups who self-report a chronic disease.







A Note on Bias in Twitter Population..

"Social media over-represents certain high-risk groups such as young, urban, and in some cases minority persons. These biases are complementary to the biases in existing survey methods which skew older and less urban."

-Dredze, Mark. "Social Monitoring for Public Health." 2017.

Data Collection Process

We used Selenium and the Twitter Advanced Search Tool to collect users who self-reported chronic health conditions and SDOH risk factors in a “marker” tweet. With these user handles and tweet IDs, we used the Twitter REST API to collect profile metadata and the preceding 100 tweets on a user’s timeline.

Data was cleaned in a multi-step process. The Botometer API removed commercial or automated Twitter accounts. The Python package Carmen identified US accounts and their approximate location. A manual check on the marker tweets verified the subject’s relevance.

Finally, the Python package Demographer labeled users as male or female, and we developed a deep learning model to predict if a user was over or under 30 years.

Future Work

Collection of more data to both improve model accuracy and representativeness of sample subjects.

Development of classification model to improve identification of self-reporting users. This would improve veracity of disease groups, accuracy of risk models, automation of pipeline, and boosting of overall data volume.

Implementation of additional models to predict demographic characteristics (e.g. income, ethnicity, age ranges)
