400% of Clinical Care

Social and economic factors drive population health 4 times as much as clinical care.

Social Determinants of Health

"The conditions in the environment in which people are born, live, learn, work, play, worship, and age that affect a wide range of health, functioning, and quality-of-life outcomes and risks.""

Studies have shown that social determinants of health have a significant impact on population health, far more than clinical care or even health behaviors. Social determinants of health are broadly defined, and include:

  • Economic Stability
  • Neighboring and Physical Environment
  • Education
  • Food
  • Community and Social Context
  • Health Care System

Research

This is quickly becoming a big area of research in the health world, and many big organizations are aligning themselves to try to pour in more of their efforts towards SDOH. HealthyPeople is a large-scale decade-long effort to track national progress against specific health goals, and a focus on SDOH is now one of the new objectives for 2020.
Many health organizations - the CDC, WHO, and even individual hospitals - are now mobilizing efforts to drive better health outcomes by improving the nation’s SDOH. Much research has been done now to confirm the effects of different social circumstances on health.

The Problem

The problem is it's hard to identify at-risk "social" patients.

While it is good that we have all of these groups recognizing the concerns surround SDOH, there haven’t been that much done to help address the issues today. There are many today who suffer from this condition, and suffer silently, not knowing that they can get treatment, or not knowing even that they should be getting preventive treatment for their situation.
